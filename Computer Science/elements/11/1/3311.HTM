The specifics of initializing the stack and the conventions for passing arguments to the initial function are machine-dependent. On the Intel x86 architecture, the stack starts at high addresses and grows down, while arguments are passed on the stack. On other systems, the stack can grow upwards and/or arguments can be passed in registers
