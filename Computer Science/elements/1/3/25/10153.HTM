"To those of you" is for when you're making a statement.

"To those of you joining us in the bidding from the internet today, I wish you good luck."

"To those of you who don't know me yet.... Fuck you." - F1 driver Valterri Bottas

"For those of you" is for when you're providing a group with information- sometimes it can be used in a sarcastic manner.

"For those of you sitting on the east of the ship, keep an eye out for the rocky island off into the distance."

"For those of you who have been affected by the events of tonights programming, please ring the following helpine..."

"For those of you that WEREN'T raised by parents with millions of dollars..." (An example of something that will lead to a sarcastic comedic statement)

"To those of you" means you are speaking to the people who you've identified. "For those of you" means whatever you're saying applies to the people who you've identified.

Obviously the meaning is very, very close because generally people don't need to listen to something that doesn't apply to them.
