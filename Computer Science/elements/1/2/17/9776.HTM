All of those examples are grammatically correct. You use "than that of" when the subject is singular (salary). You use "than those of" when the subject is plural (magazines). To help clarify it, think about changing the first sentence:

the salary of a professor is higher than a secretary's salary

the salary of a professor is higher than that of a secretary

***

the salaries of professors are higher than secretaries' salaries

the salaries of professors are higher than those of secretaries.
